00 About

I spent the first two decades of my life doing whatever I thought was fun. No grand plan, no ambitions beyond becoming a lawyer and owning nice things. Then in my late teens, I realized I care deeply about life around me—and in fact, in the entire universe.

That realization sent me down a rabbit hole through animal rights, effective altruism, biosecurity policy, and eventually to where I am now: working on frontier AI diplomacy from Geneva, trying to make sure the most powerful technology ever built doesn't go sideways. I co-founded the Simon Institute for Longterm Governance to do exactly that.

But work is only one thread. I'm also building a neighborhood community in Geneva, DJing acid techno, writing to sharpen my thinking, and trying to be a better partner and friend. I believe comfort reduces agency, that everything should be possible to say and think, and that if we play our cards right, the future will be buzzing with unfathomably rich experiences.

01 The Day Job

Frontier AI Diplomacy

Co-founder of the Simon Institute for Longterm Governance. We help the US and China communicate more productively on AI governance, work with the UN and OECD, and try to ensure frontier AI development goes well for everyone.

Started with biosecurity at the Biological Weapons Convention. Pivoted to AI when we realized that's where the leverage was. Now a team of ~10, CHF 5M+ raised, operating at the intersection of technical AI safety and international policy.

02 The Project

FractalGVA

Infrastructure for tinkerers, builders, explorers, thinkers, and artists. A physical space at Route de Malagnou 37 in Geneva’s Eaux-Vives neighborhood—part workshop, part living room, part launchpad.

Fractal University, codeathons, weekly open events, castle weekends. The programming keeps evolving but the intent stays the same: create density of interesting people and see what happens.

All my life I’ve been seeking likeminded people. FractalGVA is me finally building the place I wanted to find.

05 Core Belief

Abundance

“I just want to do interesting work with exciting people that seems to have a real shot at creating a future I'd be hyped about.”

Not just avoiding catastrophe. I’m motivated by the realistic prospect of a radically better future—positive-sum games, neverending development, the full breadth of human experience made richer and more accessible.

Thinkers who shaped this: Toby Ord, Nick Bostrom, Derek Parfit, David Deutsch, Daniel Schmachtenberger, Eliezer Yudkowsky. Most progress in history was generated by a tiny fraction of the population with a tiny fraction of the tools we’ll have soon.
